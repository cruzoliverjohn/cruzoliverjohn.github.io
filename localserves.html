Project Overview

Local Serves began its journey as a web portal specifically designed for Deliverit clients in Australia. This innovative platform functioned as a central hub, connecting people directly with their cherished local restaurants, takeaway shops, and cafes. Local Serves recognized the importance of fostering a strong sense of community, and it empowered customers to support the very hospitality businesses that enriched their neighborhoods. By providing a user-friendly platform for online ordering, Local Serves not only streamlined the food ordering process but also played a crucial role in driving business towards these local establishments.

Background

Following the success of Local Serves as a website, the product owners envisioned a natural evolution into a native mobile application. Their ambition was to create a captivating and user-friendly experience that seamlessly built upon the established online ordering app they already offered. Recognizing the power of brand recognition, they desired a modern and alluring design that echoed the visual identity of the existing platform. My initial task, therefore, became crucial. It involved crafting a range of logo and branding concepts that would serve as the foundation and inspiration for the entire mobile app's visual scheme. By establishing a strong and consistent brand presence from the outset, I aimed to ensure a smooth transition for users familiar with the website while also attracting new audiences with a fresh and engaging mobile experience.

Challenges

Fueled by the success of the Local Serves website, the product owners were eager to capitalize on the momentum and launch the mobile app as quickly as possible. This aggressive timeline necessitated a concurrent design and development approach, where designers and developers worked in close collaboration. While this fostered efficiency, it left little room for error correction through traditional iterative design cycles.

Another layer of complexity was introduced by the requirement to base the app's design on the existing online ordering platform. This presented a unique challenge: striking a balance between familiarity and innovation. The design needed to feel fresh and engaging for new users, yet retain enough elements from the website to ensure a seamless transition for existing customers. This meant pushing the boundaries of the existing layout while maintaining a core user experience consistent with the established brand.

The final hurdle arose from the inherent differences between web and mobile interfaces. Functionality that worked seamlessly on the website didn't always translate well to the smaller screen and touch-based interaction paradigm of a mobile app. This necessitated a thoughtful reimagining of core features to ensure a user-friendly and intuitive mobile experience. Essentially, we had to bridge the gap between the established web platform and the new mobile environment.

Approach

To navigate the project's demanding timeline and integrate seamlessly with existing functionalities, the product design team adopted a highly collaborative approach. We fostered a close working relationship with developers and QA testers, establishing a concurrent workflow that facilitated rapid iteration and minimized errors. Daily stand-up meetings and consistent communication ensured everyone remained on the same page.

We tackled the challenges head-on by employing a range of ideation techniques. Brainstorming sessions brought together designers, engineers, and product owners. These sessions encouraged open discussion, allowing team members to build upon each other's ideas and generate a vast pool of potential solutions. Through careful refinement and evaluation, we then distilled these possibilities down to the most effective and user-centric approaches.
